Technical SEO Audit Services

Use this page as the destination for crawl, Core Web Vitals, and structured data audits as you experiment with technical SEO improvements.

Audit crawlability, indexation, and site structure

The RankRocket Digital site gives you a realistic playground for identifying and fixing technical issues. Start with classic checks: HTTP status codes, internal broken links, redirects, robots.txt directives, and XML sitemaps. Then move into deeper analysis, such as crawl depth and orphaned pages.

Evaluate Core Web Vitals and performance

Several pages intentionally include large, uncompressed hero images and render-blocking resources. Others lean on lazy loading and smaller assets. Compare these templates in Lighthouse to see how Largest Contentful Paint, First Input Delay (or Interaction to Next Paint), and Cumulative Layout Shift behave under different conditions.

When you are ready to test improvements, you can refactor CSS, defer JavaScript, or compress imagery. Record the before/after scores to simulate an audit report, just as you would deliver for a real client.

Structured data and log file-style analysis

This sandbox includes Organization, Article, Breadcrumb, and FAQ schema in different locations. Use a schema testing tool to validate that the JSON-LD blocks parse correctly and identify any warnings. Then, test how changes to headlines, dates, or URLs might affect rich result eligibility.

While you do not have access to actual server logs here, you can approximate a log-file-style review by comparing crawl exports from your favorite SEO tools. Look for patterns such as chains of redirects, parameterized URLs, or pages with thin content.

Internal linking and broken link detection

One internal link in the Technical SEO Checklist article points deliberately to a non-existent page: /services/ppc-management.html. Use this as a controlled test case to validate how your crawling tools surface broken links and how you would prioritize fixing them.

From here, you can:

  • Design internal linking rules that connect this service to relevant articles and case studies.
  • Identify low-value links that you might remove or de-emphasize.
  • Test how changing anchor text impacts the perceived topical focus of this page.

Connect technical insights to business outcomes

Technical SEO should ultimately support business goals, not just scores. Use the Analytics & Conversion Optimization service page and the GA4 placeholders to tie your technical recommendations back to engagement and conversion metrics. For example, measure whether faster templates correlate with higher form completion rates.
