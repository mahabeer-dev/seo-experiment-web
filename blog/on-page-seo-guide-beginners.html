On-Page SEO Guide for Beginners: The Ultimate Checklist

By David Miller · September 10, 2024 · Updated September 12, 2024

You can have the best backlinks in the world, but if your on-page SEO is broken, you won't rank. This guide covers the essential elements you need to optimize on every single page.

1. Title Tags

Your title tag is arguably the most important on-page ranking factor. It tells Google what your page is about.

  • Keep it under 60 characters to avoid truncation in SERPs.
  • Place your primary keyword near the beginning.
  • Make it clickable for users, not just robots.

2. Meta Descriptions

While not a direct ranking factor, meta descriptions influence Click-Through Rate (CTR). Treat them like ad copy.

Pro tip: Include a clear Call to Action (CTA) like "Learn more," "Download now," or "Read our guide."

3. Heading Tags (H1-H6)

Headings provide structure for both users and search engines. Use only one H1 per page (usually the page title).

Use H2s for main sections and H3s for subsections. This helps Google understand the hierarchy of your content.

4. URL Structure

Keep URLs short, descriptive, and keyword-rich. Avoid dynamic parameters when possible.

Good: /blog/on-page-seo-guide.html

Bad: /blog/post?id=12345

5. Internal Linking

Links connect your content and spread link equity. Always link to related articles.

For example, if you're reading this, you might also be interested in our Technical SEO Checklist or learning about Schema Markup.

6. Image Optimization

Images can slow down your site if not handled correctly. Follow these rules:

  • Compress images before uploading.
  • Use descriptive file names (e.g., on-page-seo-checklist.jpg not IMG_001.jpg).
  • Always add alt text for accessibility and SEO.

7. Content Quality & E-E-A-T

Google prioritizes content that demonstrates Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness (E-E-A-T).

Make sure your content is comprehensive, original, and written by an expert. Citing sources and linking to authoritative external sites can also help.

Conclusion

On-page SEO is an ongoing process. Use this checklist every time you publish a new page.
